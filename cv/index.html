Teaching

Literature 306: Science Fiction

Illinois Institute of Technology: Summer 2012

  • A course focusing on science fiction novels and film adaptations. Special emphasis on film analysis/critique and comparison to source material.

  • Reading list includes works by H.G. Wells, Arthur C. Clarke, William Gibson, Michael Crichton, Drew Magary, and others.

  • For more information, view the Summer 2012 course website (built using BuddyPress)

Literature 380: Shakespeare in Film

Illinois Institute of Technology: Fall 2010, Spring 2012

  • Students performed close readings of Shakespeare's plays and examined contextual material to gain insight into Shakespeare's writing. The course also included sections on the vocabulary and theory of film and stage criticism, with an emphasis on applying those concepts to both traditional and interpretive films and performances.

  • Course included a film lab and a subsidized a trip to the Chicago Shakespeare Theater

  • Institutionalized into IIT Course Catalogue

  • For more information, view the Spring 2012 course website (built using WordPress)

Literature 309: Short Fiction

Illinois Institute of Technology: Spring 2010, Spring 2012

  • This course featured selections of less frequently anthologized short fiction pieces, as well as a few classics.

  • The goal of the course was to introduce students to this genre, collectively formulate the fundamental tenets, explore the evolution of this genre over the past two centuries, and question why short fiction is still a relevant and an integral part of media we consume today.

  • For their final projects, I encouraged students to go beyond writing a term paper and several students developed collages, photography portfolios, and websites/multimedia content.

Humanities 106: Life Stories

Illinois Institute of Technology: Fall 2009, Spring 2010, Fall 2010, Fall 2011

  • A course covering the different genres of life stories (autobiography, biography, memoir, creative non-fiction).

  • In addition to introducing literary research to freshman students, this course covers historical context and philosophical concepts that allow students to engage texts critically through written work, supplemented by instructor and peer feedback at all stages of the writing process.

  • For more information, view the Fall 2011 course website (built using WordPress)

Literature 380: The Graphic Novel

Illinois Institute of Technology: Fall 2011

  • A review of comics theory and sequential art as discussed in the works of Scott McCloud and Will Eisner. Students read major contributions to the genre in the U.S. and selections on the history of related subgenres (e.g. graphic memoir, film adaptation, etc.).

  • Students researched major authors and complied biographies on our course website, a collaborative wiki.

  • Students completed a final project where they annotated two pages from a graphic novel with descriptive callouts (using an image manipulation program) and wrote a companion essay.

  • Institutionalized into IIT Course Catalogue

  • For more information, view the Fall 2012 course website (built using WikkaWiki)

Literature 380: Hard Times

Illinois Institute of Technology: Spring 2011

  • A look at historical class conflicts as portrayed in literature. Concluded the semester with a look at the global financial crisis and economic recession.

  • Students worked in teams to research contextual information on the time periods we read about and wrote individual research papers.

Literature 315: The Novel

Illinois Institute of Technology: Spring 2011

  • A course focused on the historical progression of the novel as a genre, from the proto-novels of the 18th century through postmodern and contemporary titles.

  • I provided audio feedback on response papers (in the form of .mp3 files) and we preformed in-class research activities, including a collaborative Wikipedia editing exercise.
