Teaching

Humanities 106: Life Stories

Illinois Institute of Technology: Fall 2009, Spring 2010, Fall 2010, (Fall 2011)

  • A course covering the different genres of writing (autobiography, biography, memoir, creative non-fiction) that tell individuals' stories.

  • Different iterations of my section have included readings from Benjamin Franklin, Harriet Jacobs, Frederick Douglass, Ernest Hemingway, Jack Kerouac, Martin Luther King Jr., Tim O'Brien, Dave Eggers, and David Sedaris.

  • In addition to introducing literary research to freshman students, this course covers historical context and philosophical concepts that allow students to engage texts critically through written work, supplemented by instructor and peer feedback at all stages of the writing process.

Literature 380: Hard Times

Illinois Institute of Technology: Spring 2011

  • A look at historical class conflicts as portrayed in literature. Students read and researched novels concerning times of economic difficulty and political upheaval, and worked in teams to research contextual information on the time periods we read about.

  • Concluded the semester with a look at the the global financial crisis and economic recession.

Literature 380: Shakespeare: Print, Screen, and Stage

Illinois Institute of Technology: Fall 2010

  • Students performed close readings of Shakespeare's plays and examined contextual material to gain insight into Shakespeare's writing. This course also included sections on the vocabulary and theory of film and stage criticism, with an emphasis on applying those concepts to both traditional and interpretive films and performances.

  • Course included a film lab and a subsidized a trip to the Chicago Shakespeare Theater

Literature 315: The Novel

Illinois Institute of Technology: Spring 2011

  • A course focused on the historical progression of the novel as a genre, from the proto-novels of the 18th century through postmodern and contemporary titles.

  • I provided audio feedback on response papers (in the form of .mp3 files) and we preformed in-class research activities, including a collaborative Wikipedia editing exercise.

Literature 380: The Graphic Novel

Illinois Institute of Technology: (Fall 2011)

  • Course will offer a review of comics theory and sequential art as discussed in the works of Scott McCloud and Will Eisner. We'll also be reading major contributions to the genre in the U.S. and looking at the history of sequential art.

  • More details to come later this summer.

Literature 309: Short Fiction

Illinois Institute of Technology: Spring 2010

  • This course featured selections of less frequently anthologized short fiction pieces, as well as a few classics.

  • The goal of the course was to introduce students to this genre, collectively formulate the fundamental tenets, explore how the evolution of this genre over the past two centuries, and question why short fiction is still a relevant and an integral part of media we consume today.

  • For their final projects, I encouraged students to go beyond writing a term paper and several students developed collages, photography portfolios, and websites/multimedia content.
